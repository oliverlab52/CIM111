How to Play the 3 Basic Chords on Guitar

Overview

We will start with the basics. We will use the numbers 1, 2, 3, 4 to represent your index, middle, ring, and pinkie fingers, respectively. Next, giving the strings some letters. The string names from thick to thin strings are E, A, D, G, B, and E. Another key point is the term "fret". A fret is each block you see on your guitar neck. Most guitar necks have dots marking the 3rd, 5th, 7th, 9th, 12th, 15th, and so on. With these three chords you will already be able to play through a lot of songs.

The "C" Chord
  1. 4th finger at the 3rd fret on the A string.
  2. 3rd finger at the 2nd fret on the D string.
  3. Leave the G string open.
  4. 1st finger at the 1st fret on the B string.
  5. Strum down starting at the A string.

The "G" Chord
  1. 2nd finger at the 3rd fret on the E string.
  2. 1st finger at the 2nd fret on the A string.
  3. Leave the D and G strings open.
  4. 3rd finger at the 3rd fret on the B string.
  5. 4th finger at the 3rd fret on the E string.
  6. Strum all 6 strings.

The "D" Chord
  1. Leave the D string open.
  2. 1st finger at the 2nd fret on the G string.
  3. 3rd finger at the 3rd fret on the B string.
  4. 2nd finger at the 2nd fret on the E string.
  5. Strum down starting at the D string.
